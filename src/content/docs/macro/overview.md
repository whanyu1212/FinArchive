**Macro analysis** is the process of identifying and weighing the economic, policy, and market forces that drive asset prices at a broad, cross-market level. For FX and commodity traders, macro is the **primary framework** — it determines the directional bias, the regime, and the relative value relationships that drive medium-to-long-term positioning.

> "Macro drivers determine long-term directional bias, whereas technical analysis assists with entry timing and risk management."
> — Macrodrivers.com

---

## The Macro Hierarchy

Different macro drivers operate at different **time horizons**. Shorter-term price action is dominated by positioning and sentiment; longer-term trends follow fundamentals.

```
  TIME HORIZON     DOMINANT DRIVER
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Minutes-Hours:   Order flow, microstructure, news reaction
  Hours-Days:      Positioning, technicals, data surprises
  Weeks-Months:    Interest rate differentials, risk sentiment
  Months-Years:    Growth divergence, inflation, current account
  Years-Decades:   Purchasing power parity (PPP), structural reform
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

## Key Macro Relationships (Quick Reference)

| Driver | FX Impact | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Rate hike (hawkish CB) | Currency **strengthens** | Fed hikes → USD rallies |
| Rate cut (dovish CB) | Currency **weakens** | ECB cuts → EUR weakens |
| GDP growth surprise (beat) | Currency **strengthens** | US NFP beat → USD rallies |
| Current account surplus | Currency **strengthens** (long term) | Germany CA surplus → EUR support |
| Inflation above target | Initially **strengthens** (rate hike expectation), then **weakens** if uncontrolled | UK 2022: GBP volatile |
| Risk-off / flight to safety | USD, JPY, CHF **strengthen** | COVID 2020: DXY surge |
| Risk-on / EM rally | USD **weakens**, high-beta currencies **rally** | 2021: AUD, EM currencies |
| Oil price spike | CAD, NOK **strengthen**; JPY, EUR **weaken** | 2022 Russia-Ukraine: CAD outperformed |

## Macro Regimes

Market regimes determine which drivers dominate and how correlations behave:

```
  REGIME 1: GOLDILOCKS (Growth up, Inflation down)
  → Risk assets rally
  → USD weakens (risk appetite)
  → EM currencies and AUD/NZD outperform
  → Commodities mixed (growth bullish, but low inflation bearish gold)

  REGIME 2: REFLATION (Growth up, Inflation up)
  → Central banks hike rates
  → Initial USD strength (rate differential)
  → Commodities rally (energy, metals)
  → Short-end rates rise, yield curve flattens

  REGIME 3: STAGFLATION (Growth down, Inflation up)
  → Worst of all worlds
  → CB policy constrained (can't cut without worsening inflation)
  → Gold rallies (negative real rates)
  → Equities suffer
  → USD can be mixed — strong vs. high-beta, weak vs. CHF/gold

  REGIME 4: DEFLATION/RECESSION (Growth down, Inflation down)
  → CB cuts aggressively
  → Risk assets collapse initially, then recover on stimulus
  → USD and JPY surge (safe haven, short carry)
  → Commodities collapse (demand destruction)
  → Yield curve steepens (front end cut, back end inflation premium reduced)
```

## The Dollar Cycle

USD is the **world's reserve currency** and acts as a **barometer of global liquidity**:

```
  USD Strengthening Drivers:
  ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ Fed hiking relative to other CBs                         │
  │ Risk-off / global flight to safety                       │
  │ US current account deficit narrows                       │
  │ US exceptionalism (growth outperforms)                   │
  │ Dollar funding stress (demand for USD)                   │
  └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  USD Weakening Drivers:
  ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ Fed cutting; other CBs hiking                            │
  │ Risk-on; investors flow out of USD into EM, AUD, etc.    │
  │ US current account deficit widens                        │
  │ Fiscal expansion / debt sustainability concerns          │
  │ Global central bank diversification away from USD        │
  └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  USD cycle:
  Strong → peaks when Fed is most hawkish and growth differential highest
  Weak   → troughs when Fed starts cutting and global growth recovers
```

## Cross-Asset Correlation Framework

Macro-driven correlations between asset classes (these shift with regime):

```
  TYPICAL CORRELATIONS (Risk-off regime):
  ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  USD    ─── ↑ (safe haven)                                   │
  │  JPY    ─── ↑ (safe haven, carry unwind)                     │
  │  CHF    ─── ↑ (safe haven)                                   │
  │  Gold   ─── ↑ (safe haven, negative real rates)              │
  │  Equities ─ ↓ (risk off)                                     │
  │  Crude Oil ─ ↓ (demand collapse)                             │
  │  AUD/NZD ── ↓ (risk-beta currencies sold)                    │
  │  EM FX  ─── ↓ (capital flight to safety)                    │
  └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  TYPICAL CORRELATIONS (Risk-on regime):
  ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  USD    ─── ↓ (selling safe haven)                           │
  │  JPY    ─── ↓ (carry trade funded in JPY)                    │
  │  AUD/NZD ── ↑ (growth proxy currencies rally)                │
  │  EM FX  ─── ↑ (capital inflows)                              │
  │  Equities ─ ↑                                                │
  │  Crude Oil ─ ↑ (demand optimism)                             │
  │  Gold   ─── mixed (safe haven premium reduced)               │
  └──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Key Data Releases to Watch

| Release | Country | Frequency | FX Sensitivity |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP)** | US | Monthly | Very High (USD, all pairs) |
| **CPI / Core CPI** | US, EU, UK | Monthly | Very High |
| **FOMC / ECB / BoE / BoJ decisions** | Multiple | ~6–8x/year | Extreme (regime-setting) |
| **GDP** | US, EU | Quarterly | High |
| **ISM / PMI** | US, EU, China | Monthly | High |
| **Retail Sales** | US | Monthly | Medium-High |
| **Trade Balance** | US, China | Monthly | Medium |
| **JOLTS / ADP** | US | Monthly | Medium |
